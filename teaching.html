  • BST 222: Basics of Statistical Inference (Teaching Assistant: Fall 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016)
    • This course provides a basic, yet thorough introduction to the probability theory and mathematical statistics that underlie many of the commonly used techniques in public health research. Topics covered include probability distributions (normal, binomial, Poisson), means, variances and expected values, finite sampling distributions, parameter estimation (method of moments, maximum likelihood), confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing (likelihood ratio, Wald and score tests).
